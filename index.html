OCSS LENS — Instructional Walkthrough Tool

LENS is designed as a coaching and growth tool, not an evaluative instrument. Walkthroughs are brief, supportive observations intended to open dialogue between teachers and instructional coaches — never to judge or rank. The language throughout this tool is intentionally observational and developmental.

Observation descriptors grounded in

  • Hattie, J. — Visible Learning (2009): teacher clarity (instructional focus & purpose) & student relationships
  • Schlechty, P. — Engaging Students (2011): levels of engagement
  • Tomlinson, C.A. — The Differentiated Classroom (2014): differentiation & access
  • Fisher & Frey — text-dependent questioning & accountable talk
  • Graham & Perin — Writing Next (2007): writing as a learning tool
  • Marzano, R. — The Highly Engaged Classroom (2011): environment & engagement
  • Bryk & Schneider — Trust in Schools (2002): relational trust
  • Rowe, M.B. — Wait Time (1972, updated)
  • Georgia ELA Standards (2024) — multimodal text as a primary text type

Coaching language grounded in

  • Knight, J. — Instructional Coaching (2007)
  • Georgia TKES framework — developmental growth language
  • Bloom's Taxonomy (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001)
  • University of Pittsburgh — Accountable Talk framework
  • Webb, N.L. — Depth of Knowledge (DOK) framework

Developed by Oglethorpe County School System · Sharon Synan, Instructional Technology & Assessment Coordinator
